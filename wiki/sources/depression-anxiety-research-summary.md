# Depression and Anxiety: Psychedelic Clinical Research Summary

## Research Overview

Clinical research on psychedelics for depression and anxiety has accelerated significantly, with multiple compound classes (psilocybin, ayahuasca, 5-MeO-DMT) showing promise for treatment-resistant populations where conventional antidepressants fail.

## Key Research Areas

### 1. Psilocybin for Treatment-Resistant Depression

**Compass Pathways Phase 2/3 Trials**
- Largest randomized controlled trial of psilocybin for TRD (n=233)
- Single 25mg session with psychological support
- Significant dose-dependent reduction in MADRS scores
- FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation
- Phase 3 ongoing (2024-2025)

**Imperial College London (Robin Carhart-Harris)**
- Open-label studies in treatment-resistant depression
- Brain imaging showing DMN modulation
- Rapid and sustained effects

**Key mechanism**: 5-HT2A agonism → DMN disruption → reset of rumination patterns + neuroplasticity

### 2. Ayahuasca for Depression

**Brazilian Clinical Trials (USP, UNICAMP, UFBA)**
- Randomized controlled trial (n=29): Significant MADRS reduction vs placebo
- Pilot study (n=6): Effects maintained at 14 days
- Long-term follow-up: Effects sustained 90-180 days with integration

**Key mechanisms**:
- DMT (5-HT2A) + beta-carbolines (MAO inhibition)
- DMN modulation + anti-inflammatory effects (IL-6, CRP reduction)
- Emotional processing (amplification vs suppression in SSRIs)

### 3. 5-MeO-DMT for Depression: Phase 1/2 Trials

**Lead Researchers**: Johannes Reckweg, Alan Davis, Natalie Uthaug

**Phase 1/2 Trial Results**:
- Vaporized 5-MeO-DMT (GH001 formulation): 2-18mg doses
- Treatment-resistant depression: Safe and well-tolerated
- Dose-dependent subjective effects
- Rapid ego dissolution (15-20 min duration)

**Unique features**:
- Very high 5-HT1A affinity (content-free experiences)
- Short session duration (cost-effective, more accessible)
- Neural oscillation (gamma) increases during experience

**Alcohol Use Disorder study (Addiction Biology 2024)**:
- Stephan Tap et al.: Ego dissolution correlates with drinking reduction
- 5-MeO-DMT may work through different pathway than ayahuasca/psilocybin

### 4. Anti-inflammatory Mechanisms

Depression increasingly understood as inflammatory disease:
- Elevated IL-6, CRP, TNF-α in depressed patients
- Kynurenine pathway activation from inflammation
- Psychedelics reduce peripheral inflammatory markers:
  - Ayahuasca: IL-6 and CRP reduction correlated with antidepressant effect
  - Psilocybin: Acute inflammatory response followed by reduction
  - 5-MeO-DMT: IL-6 decrease, cortisol modulation

**Mechanism pathway**: 5-HT2A activation → shifted tryptophan metabolism → reduced neuroinflammation + increased BDNF → neuroplasticity

### 5. Comparison: Ayahuasca vs. Antidepressants

| Factor | Ayahuasca | SSRIs |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Response rate (TRD) | ~60-70% | ~15-20% after 2 failures |
| Onset | Days to weeks | 4-6 weeks |
| Dosing | 1-3 sessions (months apart) | Daily for years |
| Mechanism | 5-HT2A + MAO inhibition | Serotonin reuptake inhibition |
| Emotional effect | Amplification | Blunting/suppression |
| Effect duration | Months to year+ | Only while medicated |

**Why ayahuasca works when SSRIs fail**:
1. Different mechanism targets DMN directly
2. Allows emotional processing rather than suppression
3. Creates meaning and perspective shifts
4. "Reset" vs. continuous management

### 6. End-of-Life Anxiety and Psychedelics

**Johns Hopkins Research (Roland Griffiths)**
- Psilocybin for cancer patients with anxiety/depression
- Sustained decreases maintained at 6 months
- Mystical experience quality predicts outcome

**Ayahuasca research (PMC12449329)**
- Regular users show lower death anxiety
- Ego dissolution → acceptance of impermanence

### 7. Microdosing for Depression and Anxiety

**Evidence Level**: Weak (mostly observational)
- Nature Scientific Reports (Rootman 2021): Self-reported improvements
- No controlled trials demonstrating efficacy beyond placebo
- Difficult to blind sub-hallucinogenic doses

**Protocols**:
- Fadiman: 1 day on, 2 days off (0.1-0.3g dried mushrooms)
- Stamets: 4 days on, 3 days off (with Lion's Mane stack)

**Ayahuasca microdosing**: Less researched, complex due to beta-carbolines
