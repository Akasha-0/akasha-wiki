# Ayahuasca Neuroscience: Research Summary

## Overview

Ayahuasca activates a complex neurobiological cascade involving multiple receptor systems, signaling pathways, and brain networks. This summary covers the core neuroscience mechanisms that explain its therapeutic effects.

## 1. Receptor 5-HT2A: The Primary Gateway

### Molecular Cascade

When DMT binds to 5-HT2A receptors, a intracellular signaling cascade is triggered:

```
DMT + 5-HT2A receptor
    ↓
Protein G activation (Gs/Gq)
    ↓
Adenylate cyclase stimulation → ↑ cAMP
    ↓
PKA activation (Protein Kinase A)
    ↓
CREB phosphorylation
    ↓
Gene expression → ↑ BDNF, other neurotrophic factors
    ↓
Neuroplasticity: new synaptic connections
```

### Key Pathway: BDNF/TrkB/mTOR

The neurotrophic cascade involves:

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **BDNF** (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) | Key neurotrophin promoting neuron survival, synaptic plasticity |
| **TrkB** (Tropomyosin receptor kinase B) | BDNF receptor, triggers Akt and ERK pathways |
| **mTOR** (mammalian Target of Rapamycin) | Master regulator of protein synthesis, synaptic plasticity |
| **PI3K/Akt** | Pro-survival signaling pathway |
| **MAPK/ERK** | Cell growth, differentiation, memory consolidation |

### Intracellular 5-HT2A Receptors

Vargas et al. (2023) demonstrated that DMT acts on **intracellular 5-HT2A receptors**:

- DMT is lipophilic → crosses cell membranes
- Acts on intracellular receptor pools (not just surface)
- This explains DMT's unique psychoplastogenic properties
- Different from classical psychedelics that act primarily extracellularly

## 2. Default Mode Network (DMN) Suppression

### DMN Function

The Default Mode Network is responsible for:
- Sense of separate self
- Autobiographical memory
- Self-referential thinking
- Maintaining the "narrative ego"

### How Ayahuasca Affects DMN

```
DMN active = ego narrative in foreground
    ↓
Ayahuasca reduces DMN activity
    ↓
Reality filter diminishes
    ↓
Access to non-ordinary states increases
    ↓
Temporary ego dissolution
    ↓
Experience of unity with cosmos
```

### Neural Correlates

- Decreased activity in medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC)
- Reduced connectivity in DMN hubs
- Decreased "internal narrative"
- Increased global brain connectivity

## 3. REBUS Model (Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics)

### Bayesian Brain Framework

The brain as a prediction machine:
- **Priors**: Pre-existing beliefs about self and world
- **Prediction errors**: Mismatch between predictions and sensory input
- **Precision weighting**: Confidence assigned to predictions vs. evidence

### How Psychedelics "Relax" Priors

REBUS proposes:
1. Psychedelics reduce precision weighting of top-down predictions
2. Bottom-up sensory information gains access
3. Beliefs can be revised based on actual experience
4. Self-boundaries temporarily dissolve

### Clinical Relevance

- Rigid priors in depression/anxiety: "I am worthless", "the world is dangerous"
- Psychedelics weaken these priors → new perspectives can form
- Quality of mystical/ego-dissolution experience predicts outcomes

## 4. Entropic Brain Theory

### Conceptual Framework

Carhart-Harris (2014) proposed that psychedelics increase brain entropy:

- **Low entropy**: Rigid, predictable patterns (dominated by priors)
- **High entropy**: Flexible, randomized patterns (access to new connections)

### What Happens During Psychedelic State

```
HIERARCHICAL BRAIN:
- High order (cortical) = predominantly deterministic (low entropy)
- Low order (subcortical) = more probabilistic (high entropy)

PSYCHEDELIC STATE:
- High-level priors weakened
- Subcortical activity released
- Global connectivity increased
- Patterns become more unpredictable
```

### Correlation with Subjective Experience

- Entropy increase correlates with subjective intensity
- High entropy states = ego dissolution, mystical experiences
- The "anarchic break" when constraints fully dissolve

## 5. Neural Oscillations: Gamma and Theta

### Gamma Oscillations (38-40 Hz)

- **Associated with**: Ego-dissolution, meditation self-transcendence
- **Location**: Fronto-parietal regions
- **Interpretation**: Neurological correlate of "narrative inflection point"
- Ego dissolution marker

### Theta Oscillations (6-8 Hz)

- **Associated with**: Anxiolytic effects, relaxed alertness, cognitive control
- **Location**: Frontal midline regions (PFC, ACC)
- **Interpretation**: State of relaxed alertness conducive to mental health
- Improved inhibitory control, reduced craving

### Coherence (Cross-frequency)

- Meaning: Degree of coupling between frequency bands
- Interpretation: Functional connectivity indicator
- **Clinical relevance**: Reorganization of executive control networks

### Research Findings (5-MeO-DMT Studies)

- Acute increases in gamma → ego-dissolution marker
- Acute increases in theta → cognitive control improvement
- Reversible increases in coherence → network reorganization
- Long-term abstinent alcoholics show higher theta/alpha coherence

## 6. Sigma-1 Receptor: Neuroprotection and Anti-inflammatory

### Discovery

- Discovered in 2009 by Fontanilla et al.
- Originally mistaken for opioid receptor
- Now known as orphan receptor (binds synthetic compounds, not opioid peptides)
- Acts as transmembrane chaperone

### DMT as Endogenous Ligand

DMT is an endogenous ligand for sigma-1:

1. DMT binds sigma-1 at ER membrane
2. Complex stabilizes IP3 receptor-gated calcium channels
3. Intracellular calcium increases
4. Neuroprotective signaling cascades activated (PI3K/Akt, MAPK/ERK)
5. Anti-apoptotic gene expression, neurotrophic factors

### Neuroprotective Functions

- Controls anti-inflammatory responses
- Promotes cell survival
- Facilitates neuronal differentiation
- Protects against oxidative stress

### Neurogenesis

Morales-Garcia et al. (2020): DMT promotes adult hippocampal neurogenesis via sigma-1:
- Proliferation of neural stem cells
- Migration of neuroblasts
- Generation of new neurons

### Therapeutic Implications

- Neurodegenerative diseases (Alzheimer's models)
- Chronic neuroinflammation
- Neuropsychiatric disorders with inflammatory component

## 7. Endogenous DMT Production in Brain

### Biosynthesis Pathway

```
Tryptophan → N-methyltryptamine (NMT) → DMT
                ↑
           Enzyme INMT
    (Indole-N-methyltransferase)
```

### INMT Location

- Pineal gland (primates)
- Retinal ganglion neurons
- Spinal cord
- Human cerebral cortex
- Choroid plexus

### Receptor Targets

- **5-HT2A**: Classic hallucinogenic effects
- **Sigma-1**: Cellular stress response modulation
- **TAAR1**: Trace amine-associated receptor
- **SERT/VMAT2**: Substrate for transport

### Functional Significance

Dean et al. (2019) showed DMT levels in mammalian brain are comparable to conventional neurotransmitters.

### The "Why" of Endogenous DMT

The persistence of DMT throughout mammalian evolution and its presence at consciousness transition points (birth, death, REM sleep) suggests functional significance beyond mere metabolite.

## 8. Psychoplastogen Concept

### Definition

Psychoplastogens are substances that directly and rapidly change brain structure and function.

### DMT as Psychoplastogen

- Promotes dendritic growth
- Facilitates synapse formation
- Induces rapid structural neuroplasticity

### Plasticity Window

After psychoplastogen administration, a "plasticity window" opens (2-4 weeks):
- Brain in state of heightened neural reorganization
- Optimal time for psychotherapy integration
- Critical period for memory reconsolidation
- Opportunity to break addictive patterns

## 10. Brain Regions Affected

1. **Medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC)** - Self and cognition
2. **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)** - Emotional regulation
3. **Amygdala** - Fear processing and emotional memory
4. **Insula** - Body integration and empathy
5. **Occipital cortex** - Visual processing
6. **Basal ganglia** - Movement, reward, sleep

## Therapeutic Implications

### The "Plasticity Window" Model

```
ACUTE STATE (4-8h):
├── 5-HT2A activated
├── DMN reduced (ego dissolution)
├── Amygdala actively processing
└── Insula integrating bodily experience
         ↓
PLASTICITY WINDOW (days to weeks):
├── Elevated BDNF
├── New connections possible
└── Neural network rearrangement
         ↓
INTEGRATION (requires support):
├── Without integration: plasticity reverses
└── With integration: changes consolidate
```

### Why Integration Is Essential

Ayahuasca creates the window - but without integration work, plasticity is not used and old patterns return.
