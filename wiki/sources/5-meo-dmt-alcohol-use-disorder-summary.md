# 5-MeO-DMT for Alcohol Use Disorder: Mechanisms of Action

## Summary

This paper by Stephan C. Tap (2024) provides a first comprehensive review of 5-methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine (5-MeO-DMT) as a potential treatment for alcohol use disorder (AUD). Unlike longer-acting psychedelics like psilocybin (4-12h), 5-MeO-DMT produces rapid mystical experiences lasting only 15-20 minutes, potentially making it more cost-effective for clinical implementation. The paper synthesizes evidence suggesting that 5-MeO-DMT induces mystical experiences and ego-dissolution that correlate with reduced AUD symptoms, primarily by alleviating psychiatric mood-related comorbidities.

## Key Findings

### Alcohol Use Disorder Context
- 32.6 million people in the United States have AUD
- Treatment gap increased from 78.1% (2004) to 82.7% (2021)
- Few new medications approved in past 20 years
- Current treatments show modest efficacy with high relapse rates

### Why 5-MeO-DMT?
- Rapid onset and short duration (15-20 min vs 4-12h for psilocybin)
- High binding affinity for 5-HT1A receptor (explains lack of visual effects)
- Produces mystical experiences and ego-dissolution
- Increases psychological flexibility and mindfulness

### 5-MeO-DMT Experience
The subjective experience is distinct from other psychedelics:
- Feelings of transcendence and nondual awareness
- Often involves ego-dissolution
- Commonly described as "all-white light" or "all-black"
- Sensory deprivation: "emptiness," "nothingness," or "void"
- Reports of love, unity, awe, but also panic and terror
- Oroc's framework: 7 stages from dissolution to return

### Potential Therapeutic Mechanisms

**1. Mystical Experience**
- 66% (n=340/515) of respondents showed improvements in alcoholism
- Intensity of mystical experience significantly associated with reduction in alcohol consumption (p=0.03)
- Mediates therapeutic efficacy by alleviating mood-related comorbidities

**2. Ego-Dissolution**
- Correlates with mystical experience
- Triggers psychological change through "changes of the sense of self"
- Challenges negative self-perception (high-level Bayesian priors)
- Reduces public self-consciousness
- Increases self-compassion

**3. Mindfulness and Psychological Flexibility**
- Increases nonjudgement (associated with MDD, anxiety, stress improvements)
- Increases psychological flexibility strongly associated with PTSD, MDD, anxiety improvements
- Reduces experiential avoidance (opposite of acceptance)
- Aligns with negative reinforcement/self-medication framework

**4. Neural Oscillations (EEG findings)**
- **Gamma (38-40 Hz)**: Acute increases in fronto-parietal regions → neurological correlate of ego-dissolution
- **Theta (6-8 Hz)**: Increases associated with anxiolytic effects, relaxed alertness, cognitive control
- **Coherence across frequencies**: Reorganization of executive control networks → improved emotion regulation and inhibition

**5. Neurobiological Mechanisms (Animal Studies)**
- **Neuroplasticity**: Increases dendritic spine density in PFC (lasting 1 month), neurogenesis in hippocampus
- **Anti-inflammation**: Inhibits NF-κB signaling, reduces pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α)
- **5-HT2A agonism**: Primary mechanism for antidepressant effects
- **mGluR5 downregulation**: Implicated in rewarding effects of alcohol

## Comparison with Ayahuasca

| Aspect | 5-MeO-DMT | Ayahuasca |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Duration | 15-20 min | 6-8 hours |
| Visual effects | Minimal/absent | Present |
| 5-HT1A affinity | Very high | Lower |
| Source | Incilius alvarius toad, plants | Banisteriopsis caapi + Psychotria viridis |
| MAOi component | No | Yes (harmala alkaloids) |
| Primary receptor | 5-HT1A, 5-HT2A | 5-HT2A |

## Clinical Implications for AUD

The self-medication hypothesis suggests individuals drink to alleviate negative affect. 5-MeO-DMT may treat AUD by:
1. Reducing comorbid depression and anxiety (Mendelian randomization supports causal relationship)
2. Improving executive control and inhibitory regulation
3. Challenging rigid negative self-perceptions
4. Increasing psychological flexibility to reduce experiential avoidance

## Recommendations for Future Research
- Use robust AUD assessment tools (AUDIT, Timeline Follow-Back, Penn Alcohol Craving Scale)
- Investigate dose-response relationships for mystical intensity
- Explore Buddhist-derived frameworks (Refuge Recovery, Recovery Dharma)
- Conduct multimodal neuroimaging with pre-post assessments
- Investigate TrkB/mTor signaling pathways for neuroplasticity
