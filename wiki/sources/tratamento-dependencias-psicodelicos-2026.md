# Tratamento de Dependências com Psicodélicos 2025-2026

## Summary

This source synthesizes recent 2025-2026 research on psychedelic-assisted treatment for substance use disorders. Findings indicate that classic psychedelics (ayahuasca, psilocybin, LSD) and related compounds (5-MeO-DMT, ibogaine) demonstrate therapeutic potential across multiple addiction types, with strongest evidence for alcohol use disorder and smoking cessation. Key mechanisms include ego-dissolution, mystical experience, neuroplasticity, and modulation of reward circuitry. However, regulatory barriers and need for specialized training remain significant challenges to clinical implementation.

## Key Research Findings by Substance

### Ayahuasca for Addiction

**Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD)**
- Narrative reviews (2025) document ayahuasca's therapeutic potential for AUD
- Traditional community rituals show reduction in alcohol consumption
- Mechanisms: processing of underlying emotional trauma, ego-dissolution enabling identity change
- Self-medication hypothesis: individuals drink to alleviate negative affect; ayahuasca addresses root causes

**Smoking Cessation**
- Systematic reviews identify ayahuasca among classic psychedelics with "preliminary therapeutic potential" for smoking reduction
- Retreatment programs show significant quit rates compared to conventional methods
- Mechanism: ego-dissolution allows re-perception of smoker identity

**Cocaine Addiction (Animal Study 2025)**
- Ayahuasca "prevents the reinstatement of cocaine-induced rewarding effects" in mice (October 2025)
- Suggests anti-addiction mechanisms at neurobiological level
- Potential for harm reduction and relapse prevention

### Psilocybin for Addiction

**Smoking Cessation**
- 2025 systematic review: psilocybin associated with "significant reductions in smoking cessation"
- Qualitative studies show treatment "appears to be acceptable" among smokers
- Johns Hopkins research: high-dose psilocybin sessions produce sustained abstinence rates
- Psychological mechanism: mystical experience correlates with treatment success

**Alcohol Use Disorder**
- Systematic review found "fewer heavy drinking days, increased abstinence rates" with psilocybin-assisted therapy
- Effect size for depression symptoms: SMD=-0.62 (psilocybin/ayahuasca/LSD combined)
- Mechanism: ego-dissolution and meaning-making shift relationship with alcohol

**Acceptability in Special Populations**
- Study in people with HIV who smoke: treatment "appears to be acceptable"
- Calls for further research to evaluate safety and effectiveness

### 5-MeO-DMT for Alcohol Use Disorder

**Key Reference**: Tap (2024) - Addiction Biology

**Clinical Evidence**
- 66% of respondents (n=340/515) showed improvements in alcoholism after 5-MeO-DMT mystical experience
- Reduction in alcohol consumption significantly associated with mystical experience intensity (p=0.03)
- Case studies show reduced alcohol craving sustained at 1-month follow-up

**Mechanisms**
- Mystical experience and ego-dissolution
- Increased psychological flexibility and mindfulness
- Neural oscillations: gamma (38-40 Hz) and theta (6-8 Hz) increases
- Neuroplasticity: dendritic spine density increases lasting 1 month
- Anti-inflammatory via NF-κB pathway inhibition

**Advantages**
- Short duration (15-20 min) vs. 4-12h for psilocybin
- More cost-effective for clinical implementation
- High 5-HT1A affinity produces content-free experience

### Ibogaine for Addiction

**Research Status (2025-2026)**
- 16 total publications identified, 14 from 2025, 2 from 2026
- Accelerating research interest in therapeutic applications

**Clinical Outcomes**
- Studies focus on withdrawal symptoms and craving reduction
- Potential for opioid use disorder treatment

**Safety Concerns**
- 2026 scoping review addresses "cardiac safety" and "dose-response relationships"
- Cardiotoxicity remains documented concern requiring monitoring
- Not currently suitable for unsupervised use

**Mechanisms**
- SERT (serotonin transporter) modulators
- Neuroplastic mechanisms similar to other psychedelics

## Mechanisms of Action Across Compounds

### Koob-Volkow Three-System Model

All psychedelic-assisted treatments work by modulating three interconnected brain systems disrupted in addiction:

1. **Incentive Salience (Basal Ganglia)**
   - Hyperactivity of reward circuits
   - Psychedelics reduce compulsive "wanting"

2. **Negative Affect (Extended Amygdala)**
   - Chronic dysphoria and emotional pain
   - Psychedelics reduce negative emotional state through mystical experience

3. **Executive Function (Prefrontal Cortex)**
   - Impaired inhibitory control
   - Neural oscillations may reset executive control networks

### Common Therapeutic Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Ayahuasca | Psilocybin | 5-MeO-DMT | Ibogaine |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-----------|----------|
| Ego-dissolution | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |

## Research Gaps and Limitations

### Current Limitations
- Most studies are observational or preclinical
- Need for randomized placebo-controlled trials
- Regulatory barriers impede clinical implementation
- Specialized training requirements for therapists

### Call for Future Research
- Dose-response relationships for mystical intensity
- Biomarkers predicting treatment response
- Long-term outcomes beyond 3-6 months
- Optimal integration protocols

## Clinical Implications

### Treatment Integration
- Psychedelic-assisted therapy for addiction requires:
  - Pre-treatment preparation (set)
  - Controlled environment (setting)
  - Post-treatment integration support
  - Ongoing psychological support

### Promising Programs
- Cottonwood Tucson (9-month residential)
- Tropical Liss (ayahuasca + meditation)
- MAPS protocols for psilocybin
