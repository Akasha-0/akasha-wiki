# Considerações Legais para Ayahuasca no Brasil

## Overview

Brazil has a unique legal landscape for ayahuasca, being one of the few countries where the practice is legal and recognized. The brew is legal when used within traditional contexts, and there is a regulated system for religious and therapeutic use.

## Legal Framework

### Constitutional Status

- Ayahuasca (DMT) is legal in Brazil
- Protected under religious freedom provisions
- Regulated by CONAD (Conselho Nacional de Políticas sobre Drogas)

### Key Regulations

**Authorized Religious Groups**:
- Santo Daime (ICEERS, CEFLURIS)
- UDV (União do Vegetal)
- Barquinha (Fundação barsanilista)

**Requirements for Legitimate Practice**:
- Affiliation with recognized religious organization OR
- Traditional cultural context
- Non-commercial purpose (in most interpretations)
- Indigenous community context

## Practice Pathways in Brazil

### Religious/Traditional Context

- Join an established tradition (Santo Daime, UDV, Barquinha)
- Participate in ceremonies as a member
- Develop relationship with the tradition over time
- No formal "certification" required within traditions

### Therapeutic Context

- No formal legal framework for psychedelic therapy as of 2026
- Some practitioners operate in "gray area" with integration services
- Growing interest in regulation similar to other countries
- Risk of legal challenges for unauthorized therapeutic practice

### Research Context

- DMT/ayahuasca research permitted with proper approvals
- CEP/CONEP approval required for academic studies
- ANVISA involvement for clinical trials
- Growing number of approved research protocols

## Considerations for Practitioners

### For Brazilian Practitioners

1. **Traditional Affiliation**: Working within or with recognized traditions provides legal protection
2. **Integration Services**: Providing integration (without administering ayahuasca) carries less risk
3. **Documentation**: Keep records of training, supervision, and practice context
4. **Insurance**: Professional liability insurance recommended where available
5. **Scope of Practice**: Clearly define services and avoid claims beyond expertise

### For International Practitioners

1. **Visa Considerations**: Work authorization requirements
2. **Practice Restrictions**: Legal limitations on what foreigners can do
3. **Cultural Exchange**: Frame practice within learning and cultural exchange
4. **Export Regulations**: Transporting ayahuasca across borders is generally illegal

## Emerging Landscape (2025-2026)

### Brazilian Regulatory Reassessment (2025)

A recent reassessment of Brazilian ayahuasca regulation (Tandfonline, 2025) is considering potential changes:

- **Historical context**: Brazil was pioneer in regulating religious ayahuasca use (since 1987)
- **Current protection**: Religious use protected under religious freedom provisions
- **Reassessment focus**: Evaluating whether existing regulations remain appropriate for evolving contexts
- **Research implications**: Duke scholars note that "these churches are now providing universities conducting this research" unprecedented access to natural use contexts

### Trends to Watch

- Potential development of therapeutic practice regulations
- Growing professionalization of integration services
- International harmonization efforts
- Insurance and liability frameworks
- Telehealth integration practices
- Impact of 2025 reassessment on research and practice

### Risks

- Practice outside recognized traditions could face legal challenges
- Commercial therapeutic services remain in gray area
- International legal variations create complexity

## Ethical Considerations

- Cultural respect and reciprocity
- Appropriate scope of practice
- Integration with healthcare system
