# Políticas Psicodélicos - Comparativo Internacional

## Overview

Different countries and jurisdictions have taken varied approaches to ayahuasca and psychedelic regulation. Understanding these frameworks is essential for practitioners, researchers, and those seeking treatment.

## International Comparison

### Ayahuasca Legal Status by Region

| Region | Status | Framework |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Brazil** | Legal for religious use | Religious freedom since 1987 |
| **Peru** | Legal (traditional context) | Traditional use recognized |
| **Colombia** | Legal (traditional context) | Traditional practices protected |
| **USA** | DMT/Syrian rue: Schedule I | Federal prohibition |
| **Canada** | DMT: Schedule I | Federal prohibition |
| **EU** | Varies by country | Generally prohibited |
| **Australia** | Scheduled | Therapeutic access limited |

### USA: Oregon and Colorado (2020-2026)

**Oregon Measure 109 (2020)**:
- Psilocybin services legalized for supervised therapeutic use
- Oregon Health Authority regulates licensed facilities
- Available for adults 21+ in clinical settings
- First state to legalize psychedelic therapy

**Colorado Proposition 122 (2022)**:
- Decriminalized psilocybin and psilocin
- Created natural medicine therapy program
- Allows healing centers starting 2025
- Process similar to Oregon but with some differences

**Key Distinction from Ayahuasca**:
- Oregon/Colorado frameworks apply to **psilocybin** (not ayahuasca)
- Ayahuasca remains Schedule I at federal level
- Religious use exemptions exist case-by-case (Santo Daime in US)

### Ayahuasca-Specific Comparison

| Aspect | Ayahuasca | Psilocybin (Oregon/CO) |
|--------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Evidence** | Moderate (emerging) | Strong (Phase III) |
| **Legal - Brazil** | Religious use legal | Off-label possible |
| **Legal - USA** | Not federally legal | Legal (Oregon/CO only) |
| **Risks** | MAOi + meds, cardiovascular | Psychosis, bad trips |
| **Cost** | $500-3000 (retreat) | $2500-15000 (clinical) |
| **Access** | High in Brazil | Moderate (Oregon/CO) |

## Brazilian Context

Brazil occupies a unique position:
- **Pioneer**: First country to regulate religious ayahuasca use (1987)
- **Protected**: Religious use protected under constitutional religious freedom
- **Research hub**: Growing number of approved research protocols
- **International model**: Often cited as model for other countries

### 2025 Regulatory Reassessment

A recent reassessment (Tandfonline, 2025) is evaluating:
- Whether current regulations remain appropriate
- Therapeutic use frameworks being considered
- Impact on international research collaborations

## Ethical Considerations

### Before Crossing Borders

1. **Research local laws thoroughly** - penalties vary widely
2. **Understand exemptions** - religious use exceptions are specific
3. **Consult legal professionals** - especially for therapeutic practice
4. **Consider insurance implications** - coverage varies by jurisdiction

### For Practitioners

- Operating outside recognized frameworks risks legal consequences
- Frameworks protect practitioners and participants
- Documentation and informed consent essential
