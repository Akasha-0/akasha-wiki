# Caminho de Formação do Terapeuta Psicodélico

## Overview

This guide outlines the typical pathways for becoming a psychedelic therapist, with specific considerations for those focusing on ayahuasca. The journey combines academic training, experiential learning, and personal development.

## Typical Pathway

### Phase 1: Foundation (1-2 years)

**Academic Background**
- Undergraduate degree in psychology, counseling, social work, or related field
- Healthcare or therapeutic background
- Alternative: relevant life experience with demonstrated aptitude

**Personal Development**
- Regular meditation or mindfulness practice
- Personal therapy or coaching
- Exploratory experiences with plant medicines (where legally permitted)

**Initial Training**
- Mental health first aid
- Trauma-informed care certification
- Basic counseling skills

### Phase 2: Specialized Training (1-2 years)

**Professional Certification Programs**

| Program | Duration | Hours | Location |
|---------|----------|-------|----------|
| CIIS | 10 months | 140-150 | San Francisco, USA |
| Synthesis Institute | 13 months + 12 months practicum | ~200 | Amsterdam, Netherlands |
| Polaris | varies | varies | Canada |
| MAPS | 6 days intensive | Intensive | International (Italy, Mexico 2026) |

**Typical Curriculum Includes**
- Screening and assessment
- Set and setting principles
- Facilitation skills
- Integration practices
- Ethics and legal considerations
- Supervised clinical hours

**Complementary Training**
- MBSR (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction)
- Somatic experiencing
- Trauma-focused therapies (EMDR, etc.)
- Family systems work

### Phase 3: Specialization (ongoing)

**Areas of Focus**
- Trauma and PTSD
- Grief and loss
- Addiction recovery
- End-of-life care
- Spiritual emergence
- Depression and anxiety

**Advanced Training**
- Supervision and mentorship
- Advanced integration training
- Research participation
- Specialized populations

## Considerations for Ayahuasca-Specific Work

### Brazilian Context

For those planning to work in Brazil:

1. **Legal Framework**: Understand CONEA/SENAD regulations
2. **Traditional Knowledge**: Learn from established traditions (Santo Daime, UDV, Barquinha)
3. **Apprenticeship**: Consider traditional apprenticeship alongside formal training
4. **Integration of Approaches**: Combine traditional practices with evidence-based methods

### International Considerations

For ayahuasca facilitators:

- Destination country regulations
- Tourism vs. local community practice
- Cultural appropriation concerns
- Benefit-sharing with traditional communities

## Cost Structure Overview

| Component | Estimated Cost |
|-----------|---------------|
| Undergraduate degree | $20,000-100,000+ |
| Professional training | $5,000-15,000 |
| Personal therapy | $2,000-10,000/year |
| Supervision | $1,000-5,000/year |
| Continuing education | $500-2,000/year |

## Time Commitment

- Professional training programs: 140-200 hours over 9-13 months
- Additional supervised practice: 100-500 hours
- Personal development: ongoing throughout career
- Total to independent practice: 3-5 years typical

## Key Questions for Prospective Students

1. What is your primary population of interest?
2. Which substances do you want to work with?
3. Where do you plan to practice (jurisdiction)?
4. Do you want to integrate traditional and modern approaches?
5. What is your timeline and financial situation?
