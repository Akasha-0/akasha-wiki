# Grawe's Common Factors in Psychedelic Therapy

## Overview

Recent theoretical work (Wolff et al., 2025) has applied Grawe's model of general change mechanisms to understand how psychedelic therapy produces therapeutic effects. This framework helps explain why different psychedelic compounds can be effective for different conditions.

## Grawe's Six Change Mechanisms

Based on Klaus Grawe's psychotherapy research, the six core mechanisms are:

1. **Resolution of approach-avoidance conflict** - Overcoming motivational contradictions
2. **Processing of core conflictual relationship themes** - Working through interpersonal patterns
3. **Cognitive restructuring** - Changing maladaptive beliefs and interpretations
4. **Competence acquisition** - Developing new coping skills and abilities
5. **Motivation enhancement** - Increasing engagement and commitment to change
6. **Integration of experience** - Making meaning and incorporating insights

## Application to Psychedelic Therapy

Wolff et al. (2025) argue that psychedelics may work by:
- Temporarily disrupting default mode network activity
- Allowing previously avoidance-protected material to surface
- Enabling novel cognitive frameworks to form
- Creating conditions favorable for all six mechanisms to operate

## Key Publication

Wolff et al. "Common factors in altered states: Understanding psychedelic therapy through the lens of Grawe's general change mechanisms" - Psychological Review (2025) [OPEN ACCESS]

## Validation Tool

Wolff et al. (2024) also developed and validated the **General Change Mechanisms Questionnaire (GCMQ)** - Journal of Psychopharmacology [OPEN ACCESS]
- Measures the six mechanisms empirically
- Allows comparison across therapy types
- Applied to psychedelic therapy contexts

## Significance

This framework offers:
- A language for comparing psychedelic therapy to other evidence-based therapies
- Predictors of who will benefit
- Guidance for optimizing therapeutic support
- Bridge between mechanistic neuroscience and psychotherapy research
