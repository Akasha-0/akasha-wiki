# Competências do Terapeuta Psicodélico

## Overview

This page outlines the essential competencies required for practicing psychedelic-assisted therapy, with specific adaptations for ayahuasca work. These competencies are drawn from emerging professional standards and training programs in the field.

## Clinical Competencies

### 1. Assessment and Patient Selection

- Screening for contraindications
- Psychological readiness evaluation
- Expectation management
- Informed consent processes
- Risk assessment for specific conditions

### 2. Preparation (Set)

- Psychoeducation delivery
- Intention setting facilitation
- Rapport building
- Anxiety management techniques
- Expectation alignment

### 3. Facilitation (Session)

- Present-centered therapist awareness
- Non-directive posture
- Support during difficult experiences
- Crisis management skills
- Empathic communication in altered states
- Managing physical manifestations (purging, etc.)

### 4. Integration

- Experience processing
- Translating insights into behavioral change
- Long-term follow-up care
- Referrals when necessary
- Documentation and continuity

## Personal Competencies

- Emotional maturity and self-awareness
- Stability and presence under pressure
- Humility (recognizing limits and errors)
- Capacity to be with the unknown
- Ethical integrity
- Self-care and burnout management

## Ayahuasca-Specific Competencies

For practitioners working specifically with ayahuasca:

- Knowledge of Brazilian traditions (Santo Daime, UDV, etc.)
- Familiarity with ritual context
- Ability to work with "entity" encounters and spiritual experiences
- Understanding of icaros and music as therapeutic tools
- Management of purging (physical and emotional)
- Knowledge of MAOi interactions and contraindications
- Understanding of traditional ceremonial protocols

## Regulatory and Legal Knowledge

- Understanding of local laws regarding psychedelic practice
- Documentation requirements
- Scope of practice boundaries
- Insurance and liability considerations
- Harm reduction frameworks

## Continuing Development

Competent practitioners should engage in:

- Ongoing personal therapy or processing
- Supervision and consultation
- Continuing education in psychedelic research
- Integration of Indigenous knowledge with cultural humility
- Participation in professional communities
