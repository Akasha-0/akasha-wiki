# Comparativo de Programas de Formação em Terapia Psicodélica

## Overview

This comparison guide helps prospective students evaluate major psychedelic therapy training programs. Each program has distinct strengths and focuses, making the choice dependent on individual goals and circumstances.

## Program Comparison Table

| Aspect | CIIS | Synthesis Institute | Polaris | MAPS |
|--------|------|---------------------|---------|------|
| **Location** | San Francisco, USA | Amsterdam, Netherlands | Canada | International |
| **Duration** | 10 months | 13 months | Varies | 6 days intensive |
| **Hours** | 140-150 | ~200 | Varies | Intensive |
| **Format** | In-person | Hybrid | Varies | In-person |
| **Cost** | $5,000-15,000 | $5,000-15,000 | Varies | Varies |
| **Focus** | Broad psychedelic therapy | Facilitator training | Plant medicine integration | MDMA/psychedelic therapy |
| **Ayahuasca emphasis** | Brief | Moderate | High | None |

## CIIS (California Institute of Integral Studies)

**Best For**: Practitioners seeking comprehensive training with strong academic backing

**Strengths**:
- Established reputation and credibility
- Experienced faculty with clinical expertise
- Integration of multiple therapeutic traditions
- Strong theoretical foundation
- Network of alumni in clinical settings

**Considerations**:
- Requires advanced degree or bachelor's
- Located in USA (consider visa requirements)
- Ayahuasca coverage is brief

## Synthesis Institute

**Best For**: Those seeking European perspective and facilitator-focused training

**Strengths**:
- European regulatory perspective
- Focus on professional facilitation skills
- Personal experiential component
- Connection to Amsterdam's progressive drug policy environment
- Balanced approach to traditional and modern practices
- Multi-phase pathway: Core Training, Immersion Apprenticeship, 12-month Practicum
- Trauma-informed approach emphasized

**Considerations**:
- Located in Netherlands
- Longer duration (13 months core + 12 months practicum)
- May require additional training for specific populations
- Format: Mix of on-demand content and live components

## Polaris

**Best For**: Those interested in Indigenous wisdom and harm reduction approaches

**Strengths**:
- Deep engagement with Indigenous traditions
- Strong harm reduction framework
- Plant medicine focus
- Canadian regulatory perspective
- Integration of traditional and Western knowledge

**Considerations**:
- Program details vary
- May require additional credentialing for clinical practice
- Strong emphasis on personal readiness

## MAPS (Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies)

**Best For**: Mental healthcare providers seeking training in psychedelic-assisted therapy, especially MDMA for PTSD

**Strengths**:
- International programs (Australia, Europe, Americas)
- "Inner-directed approach" - distinctive therapeutic methodology
- Training for healthcare providers with clinical background
- Preparation, medication session, and integration session guidance
- Strong emphasis on therapeutic alliance
- Handles sensitive issues: therapeutic touch, suicidal ideation, transference/counter-transference

**Considerations**:
- Focus on MDMA-assisted therapy (not ayahuasca)
- Six-day intensive format requires significant time commitment
- Programs in 2026: Italy (September), Mexico (October in Spanish)
- Currently focused on research/clinical trial context

## Decision Framework

### Choose CIIS if:
- You want an established academic credential
- You're targeting clinical practice in the USA
- You value integration of multiple therapeutic modalities
- You have or are pursuing an advanced degree

### Choose Synthesis if:
- You prefer European training standards
- You want strong facilitator skills development
- You're interested in working in Europe
- You value the Amsterdam psychedelic community

### Choose Polaris if:
- You're particularly drawn to plant medicine traditions
- You prioritize Indigenous wisdom integration
- You prefer harm reduction frameworks
- You're training for work in Canada or similar contexts

### Choose MAPS if:
- You're a mental healthcare provider with clinical background
- You're specifically interested in MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD
- You want training based on the "inner-directed approach"
- You're preparing for clinical trial participation or research contexts
- You want to work in settings that handle trauma from conflict/disasters

## Additional Considerations

### Ayahuasca-Specific Training

For those specifically focused on ayahuasca:

1. **Supplemental Training**: Consider additional training beyond core psychedelic therapy programs
2. **Traditional Apprenticeship**: Many ayahuasca practitioners seek mentorship with experienced traditional practitioners
3. **Brazilian Programs**: Explore training options within Brazil (IBRATH, other institutions)
4. **Integration Specialization**: Focus on integration skills for ayahuasca-specific work

### Legal Considerations by Jurisdiction

| Region | Practice Status | Training Requirements |
|--------|----------------|----------------------|
| Brazil | Legal for religious use | Varies; tradition-based pathways available |
| USA | Federally illegal; state-level exceptions | No formal licensure for psychedelic therapy |
| Canada | Restricted; emerging pathways | Evolving regulatory framework |
| Netherlands | Tolerated; not fully legal | No formal professional recognition |

### Cost-Benefit Analysis

**Factors Beyond Price**:
- Reputation and network effects
- Alumni outcomes and job placement
- Supervision and mentorship availability
- Location and associated living costs
- Recognition by insurance providers
- Pathway to licensure (where applicable)
