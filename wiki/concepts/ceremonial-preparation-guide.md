# Ceremonial Preparation Guide

## Overview

This guide provides practical preparation protocols for participants and facilitators of ayahuasca ceremonies. Drawing from traditional Brazilian practices, contemporary psychedelic harm reduction research, and the ReSPCT guidelines (Nature Medicine, 2025), these recommendations support safe, transformative experiences.

### Week of Ceremony

**Physical Preparation:**
- Continue dietary restrictions
- Ensure adequate sleep
- Light, easy-to-digest meals
- Stay well-hydrated
- Avoid strenuous exercise
- No alcohol or substances

**Mental Preparation:**
- Set clear intentions
- Review your "why"
- Prepare for both challenging and blissful experiences
- Commit to staying through the full experience
- Practice acceptance and surrender
- Visualize the ceremony going well

**Logistical Preparation:**
- Arrange transportation (you won't be able to drive)
- Confirm accommodation if away from home
- Notify work/family you'll be unavailable
- Prepare arrival time
- Pack comfortable clothing
- Bring water, tissues, any comfort items

### Day of Ceremony

**Morning:**
- Light breakfast or fast
- Spend time in quiet reflection
- Set final intentions
- Avoid rushed activities
- Gentle movement or stretching

**Afternoon:**
- Light meal 4-6 hours before
- Rest and conserve energy
- Avoid work or stressful activities
- Stay in calm environment
- Avoid screens if possible
- Pray, meditate, or simply be

**Arrival:**
- Arrive early to acclimate
- Wear comfortable, loose clothing
- Remove jewelry and accessories
- Turn off phone
- Leave problems at the door
- Greet others with respect

### Post-Ceremony

**Immediate (Same Night/Morning):**
- Rest as much as possible
- Sleep if you can
- Stay warm
- Gentle fluids when ready
- Light food when hungry
- Don't make major decisions
- Allow emotions to flow

**Next Day:**
- Full rest day
- No work obligations
- Gentle activities only
- Journal about experience
- Walk in nature if feels right
- Process with trusted friend if helpful
- Avoid alcohol and substances

**Week After:**
- Maintain gentle routine
- Practice integration techniques
- Attend integration circles if available
- Be patient with yourself
- Expect emotions to surface
- Maintain boundaries around media
- Consider therapy or counseling

## For Facilitators

### Pre-Ceremony Checklist

**Space Preparation:**
- [ ] Space cleansed and consecrated
- [ ] Lighting adjusted
- [ ] Temperature comfortable
- [ ] Music system tested
- [ ] Safety hazards removed
- [ ] Comfort items available (blankets, pillows)
- [ ] Buckets/basins positioned
- [ ] Tissues, water accessible
- [ ] Bathroom clear and accessible
- [ ] First aid kit ready

**Participant Preparation:**
- [ ] Medical/psychological screening complete
- [ ] Consent forms signed
- [ ] Intentions documented
- [ ] Emergency contacts obtained
- [ ] Medication review complete
- [ ] Contraindications checked
- [ ] Dietary restrictions noted

**Facilitator Preparation:**
- [ ] Facilitator team briefed
- [ ] Roles assigned
- [ ] Communication signals established
- [ ] Emergency protocols reviewed
- [ ] Self-care practiced
- [ ] Grounding/centering complete

### In-Ceremony Protocols

**Opening:**
1. Gather participants with prayer/intention
2. Explain safety parameters
3. Establish sacred space
4. Review guidelines
5. Answer final questions
6. Distribute medicine with intention

**Monitoring:**
- Scan room regularly
- Watch for distress signs
- Respond to signals promptly
- Support without disrupting
- Maintain calm presence
- Coordinate team responses

**Distress Protocol:**

**Closing:**
1. Signal ceremony completion
2. Offer gratitude
3. Facilitate sharing if appropriate
4. Provide integration guidance
5. Ensure safe transportation
6. Schedule follow-up

### Post-Ceremony Responsibilities

- Follow-up with each participant
- Identify those needing extra support
- Document significant events
- Team debrief
- Self-care after intense sessions
- Maintenance of records
- Planning for next ceremony

## Traditional Preparation Elements

From Santo Daime, UDV, and Indigenous traditions:

### Santo Daime Preparation

- **Fardamento**: Wearing white uniform
- **Horto**: Spiritual garden preparation
- **Limpeza**: Energetic cleaning with prayers
- **Jejum**: Dietary restrictions
- **Oração**: Personal prayer practice
- **Hinários**: Singing the songs beforehand

### Indigenous Preparation

- **Dieta**: Food restrictions (often 1-4 weeks)
- **Abstinência Sexual**: Sexual restriction
- **Solitude**: Time alone in nature
- **Icaros**: Learning and practicing songs
- **Smoking mapacho**: Tobacco for protection

### UDV Preparation

- **Membro Discípulo**: Learning the principles
- **Preces**: Prayers before ceremony
- **Dieta**: Dietary restrictions
- **Mederia**: Personal spiritual preparation
- **Participação**: Active in community practices

## When Not to Proceed

### Participant Contraindications

**Absolute Contraindications:**
- Active psychosis or schizophrenia
- Severe bipolar disorder (type 1)
- Current suicidal ideation with plan
- Certain heart conditions
- MAOIs or contraindicated medications
- Pregnancy
- Unknown substances in system

**Relative Contraindications:**
- History of seizures
- Diabetes (requires medical consultation)
- High blood pressure
- History of trauma (may need extra support)
- Recent significant loss
- Current acute illness

### Situational Contraindications

- Facilitator not adequately prepared
- Space not ready
- Team incomplete
- Weather extremes
- Participant visibly intoxicated
- Emergency services not accessible
- No integration plan
