# Alcohol Use Disorder: Psychedelic Treatment Approaches

## Overview

Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD) affects 32.6 million people in the United States alone, with an 82.7% treatment gap. Conventional treatments show modest efficacy with high relapse rates. Recent research indicates that psychedelics including ayahuasca, psilocybin, and 5-MeO-DMT may offer novel mechanisms for treating AUD by addressing underlying psychological drivers, enhancing neural plasticity, and inducing transformative experiences that disrupt addiction patterns.

## The AUD Problem

### Current Treatment Landscape

```
ALCOHOL USE DISORDER: STATISTICS AND TREATMENT GAP
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

• US prevalence: 32.6 million with AUD
• Treatment gap: 82.7% (2021, increased from 78.1% in 2004)
• Conventional treatments: modest efficacy, high relapse
• Few new medications approved in past 20 years
• Relapse rates remain high across treatment modalities
```

### Limitations of Current Approaches

- Pharmacological treatments often substitute one substance for another
- Behavioral therapies address symptoms but not underlying causes
- Neither approach adequately addresses the existential/spiritual dimensions of addiction
- High comorbidity with trauma and mood disorders often unaddressed

## Psychedelic Mechanisms for AUD

### The Koob-Volkow Framework

AUD can be understood through the three-system neurocircuitry model:

```
KOOB-VOLKOW THREE-SYSTEM MODEL FOR AUD
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. INCENTIVE SALIENCE (Basal Ganglia)
   • Hyperactivity of reward circuits
   • "Wanting" vs "Liking" - compulsive drinking
   • Dopamine dysregulation

2. NEGATIVE AFFECT (Extended Amygdala)
   • Chronic dysphoria, anxiety
   • CRF, dynorphin, norepinephrine dysregulation
   • Self-medication: drinking to escape negative emotion

3. EXECUTIVE FUNCTION (Prefrontal Cortex)
   • Impaired inhibitory control
   • Cannot stop despite consequences
   • Decision-making deficits

PSYCHEDELIC ACTION:
• Ego dissolution → re-perception of behavior
• Neural oscillations → reset executive networks
• Mystical experiences → directly alter negative affect
• Neuroplasticity → reorganize addiction circuits
```

### Key Mechanisms

**1. Ego Dissolution and Identity Change**
- AUD is associated with rigid identity fusion: "I am a drinker"
- Psychedelics reduce DMN activity, allowing separation from addiction identity
- "I am not my addiction" becomes experientially real
- New identity as "person in recovery" can form

**2. Emotional Processing of Underlying Issues**
- Many with AUD self-medicate unprocessed trauma, grief, or emotional pain
- Ayahuasca allows access to and processing of root emotional material
- Reduced need for alcohol to escape discomfort
- Addresses the "why" behind drinking

**3. Neuroplasticity and Circuit Reset**
- Psychedelics elevate BDNF, opening windows of neural plasticity
- Addiction circuits can be reorganized during these windows
- New, healthier patterns can form
- Lasting changes in brain connectivity

**4. Experience of Alternative States**
- Alcohol promises relief, connection, peace
- Ayahuasca/psilocybin can induce experiences that fulfill these needs without alcohol
- "I can feel peace/alive/connected without drinking"
- Breaks the belief that alcohol is necessary

**5. Mindfulness and Psychological Flexibility**
- AUD involves inflexibility: rigid drinking patterns, experiential avoidance
- Psychedelics temporarily increase psychological flexibility
- Skills developed can transfer to post-treatment life
- Defusion from alcohol-related thoughts

## Ayahuasca for AUD

### Evidence Summary

Research on ayahuasca for AUD includes:

- Observational studies of traditional community users showing reduced alcohol consumption
- Clinical observations of reduced drinking in retreat settings
- Mechanisms parallel to those observed in 5-MeO-DMT research
- Subjective experiences suggest addressing root causes

### Mechanisms Specific to Ayahuasca

- **DMT activity at sigma-1 receptors**: neuroprotection, potential addiction-interrupting effects
- **Harmala MAOIs**: antidepressant effects, may address comorbidity
- **Long duration (6-8 hours)**: extended processing time
- **Visual/tactile effects**: may support emotional processing
- **Traditional context alignment**: ritual structure supports therapeutic outcomes

## Psilocybin for AUD

### Clinical Trials

Phase 2 trials for psilocybin-assisted treatment of AUD have shown promise:
- Single to two psilocybin sessions combined with psychotherapy
- Significant reductions in drinking days and heavy drinking days
- Mystical experience intensity correlates with better outcomes
- Effects maintained at follow-up

## 5-MeO-DMT for AUD

### Tap 2024 Study

The Addiction Biology paper (2024) provides comprehensive mechanisms:

**Key Findings:**
- 66% (n=340/515) of naturalistic 5-MeO-DMT users showed improvements in alcoholism
- Intensity of mystical experience significantly associated with reduced alcohol consumption (p=0.03)
- Mediates therapeutic efficacy by alleviating mood-related comorbidities

**Proposed Mechanisms:**
1. Mystical experience → ego dissolution → reduced addiction identity
2. Psychological flexibility increases → better emotion regulation
3. Mindfulness effects → reduced automatic drinking
4. Neural oscillations → improved executive control
5. Neuroplasticity → circuit reorganization

## Integration Approaches

### ACT-Based Integration

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy processes align with psychedelic mechanisms:
- Acceptance: allowing experiences without avoidance
- Defusion: observing thoughts rather than being them
- Values: reconnecting with what truly matters
- Committed action: behavioral changes aligned with values

### THRIVE Model

The THRIVE framework provides structured integration:
- T: Trusted space for processing
- H: Harm reduction awareness
- R: Relational support
- I: Integration of insights
- V: Values alignment
- E: Existential/spiritual processing
