# Safety Protocols for Ayahuasca Ceremonies

## Overview

Safety protocols are structured procedures designed to prevent, identify, and manage adverse events during ayahuasca ceremonies. The ReSPCT guidelines (Nature Medicine, 2025) and psychedelic harm reduction research provide frameworks for ensuring participant safety while maintaining the conditions necessary for therapeutic and spiritual benefit.

## Why Safety Protocols Matter

Unlike conventional therapeutic settings, ayahuasca ceremonies involve:
- Profound alterations in consciousness and perception
- Temporary loss of ordinary cognitive functions
- Dramatic physical symptoms (vomiting, temperature changes, heart rate elevation)
- Access to traumatic psychological material
- Potential for dangerous interactions with medications

## Pre-Ceremony Screening

### Medical Contraindications

Absolute contraindications (must exclude):

| Condition | Reason | Alternative |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **MAOIs** (including some antidepressants) |life-threatening hypertensive crisis | Wait washout period |
| **SSRIs** | Reduced ayahuasca effect; serotonin syndrome risk | Wait washout; consider alternative |
| **Severe bipolar disorder** | Mania trigger risk | Stable on medication; avoid |
| **Schizophrenia or psychosis** | Can trigger or worsen psychotic episode | Not appropriate |
| **Severe heart conditions** | Cardiovascular stress from MAOIs | Medical supervision essential |
| **Uncontrolled hypertension** | MAOI-related blood pressure crisis | Control BP first |

### Relative Contraindications (Require Assessment)

- History of seizures
- Diabetes
- Pregnancy (unknown risk)
- Breastfeeding (unknown risk)
- History ofdissociative disorders
- Current severe life crisis without support
- History of psychedelic adverse reactions

### Psychological Screening

**Red Flags Requiring Careful Assessment:**
- Active suicidal ideation with plan
- Recent bereavement (within 4 weeks)
- Active PTSD with flashback episodes
- History of psychiatric hospitalization
- Current use of alcohol or substances of abuse
- Unrealistic expectations about experience

**Protective Factors to Assess:**
- Stable housing and life situation
- Social support system
- Previous positive psychedelic experiences
- Realistic expectations
- Commitment to staying for full experience
- Willingness to do integration work

### Pharmaceutical Considerations

**Key Risk: Hypertensive Crisis**
- MAOIs + tyramine-rich foods + ayahuasca = dangerous BP elevation
- Traditional diets avoid: aged cheeses, cured meats, fermented foods, soy products
- Modern context: Screen for MAOI use including Syrian Rue

**Serotonin Syndrome Risk:**
- Combining ayahuasca with SSRIs, SNRIs, or other serotonergics is dangerous
- Washout periods required before ceremony
- Timeline depends on specific medication half-life

## Psychological Safety Protocols

### During-Ceremony Distress Management

**Recognizing Crisis Signs:**
- Expressing desire to stop or escape repeatedly
- Physical aggression or agitation
- Severe emotional flooding beyond capacity to process
- Dissociation (unresponsive, glassy-eyed)
- Reports of wanting to harm self or others

**Grounding Techniques:**
1. **Physical:** Holding hand, gentle touch on shoulder, providing structure
2. **Verbal:** Calm, reassuring voice; simple orienting statements
3. **Breath:** Guiding slow, deep breathing
4. **Orientation:** Reminding of time, place, safety
5. **Sensory:** Holding ice, water on face, grounding objects

### Managing Severe Reactions

**Difficult vs. Dangerous Experiences:**

Not all difficult experiences are dangerous - some are necessary therapeutic breakthroughs. The key distinction:

| Difficult but Potentially Therapeutic | Dangerous Requiring Intervention |
|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Crying, grief expression | Uncontrollable violence |
| Physical purging | Severe disorientation for hours |
| Confronting feared memories | Complete dissociation unresponsive |
| Expressing fear that passes | Persistent suicidal ideation |
| Emotional release followed by resolution | Panic attacks not resolving |

## Physical Safety Protocols

### Environmental Safety

**Space Setup:**
- Clear pathways between participants
- Soft landing surfaces (mats) for those who may fall
- No sharp objects or hazards
- Night lighting sufficient for navigation
- Proximity to bathrooms without isolation

**During Peak Effects:**
- Participants may lose coordination
- Some may fall unexpectedly
- Facilitators must be positioned for quick response
- Keep space clear for movement

### Food and Toilet Protocols

**Vomiting (Purging):**
- Buckets/basins for each participant positioned within reach
- Tissues, paper towels readily accessible
- Cleanup supplies available
- Ventilation to manage odors
- Spare clothing for those who need it

**Bathroom Protocol:**
- Trust participants to manage alone but establish check-ins
- Buddy system for those who may be unstable
- Facilitator available outside bathroom area
- Clear pathway with night lighting

## Post-Ceremony Safety

### Immediate Aftercare

**Same Night:**
- Observe participants as effects moderate
- Ensure safe transportation for all
- Provide light food/drink if appropriate
- No driving or operating machinery until fully baseline
- Someone should stay with particularly affected participants

**Following Days:**
- Contact check-ins for participants who had difficult experiences
- Provide integration support resources
- Be available for processing conversations
- Watch for delayed adverse reactions

## Drug Interactions: Critical Protocol

### High-Risk Combinations

| Substance | Risk | Protocol |
|-----------|------|----------|
| **MAOIs** (including Selegiline, Harmaline) | Hypertensive crisis, death | Absolute contraindication |
| **SSRIs** (Prozac, Zoloft, etc.) | Reduced effect, serotonin syndrome | Washout 2-6 weeks depending |
| **SNRIs** (Effexor, etc.) | Serotonin syndrome | Washout similar to SSRIs |
| **5-HTP** | Serotonin syndrome | Contraindication |
| **Tramadol** | Seizure risk | Contraindication |
| **Dextromethorphan** | Serotonin syndrome | Contraindication |
| **Alcohol** | Dangerous sedation, vomiting | Avoid 24+ hours before/after |

## Reporting and Documentation

### Adverse Event Documentation

According to ReSPCT guidelines, documentation should include:

1. **Physical setting details** where event occurred
2. **Social setting factors** that may have contributed
3. **Temporal factors** (time in session, number of doses)
4. **Participant demographics** relevant to risk
5. **Pre-existing conditions** and screening performed
6. **Substances used** ( ayahuascabrew characteristics, other substances)
7. **Timeline of events** leading to adverse outcome
8. **Intervention performed** and response
9. **Outcome** and follow-up actions

### When to Refer to Professional Care

**Indicators for Professional Referral:**
- Persistent psychiatric symptoms after ceremony
- Participants who reveal ongoing risk factors
- Those expressing desire to harm self after ceremony
- Participants whose experiences trigger need for ongoing therapy
- Anyone requesting continued psychedelic work who may not be appropriate

## Legal Considerations

###巴西 (Brazil) Context

Since 1992, ayahuasca is legal in Brazil for religious use by registered congregations (Santo Daime, Barquinha, UDV). However:

- Non-religious use exists in legal gray area
- Facilitators outside these traditions assume additional risk
- Export/import is illegal
- Commercial facilitation can face legal challenges

### International Context

Legal status varies dramatically:
- **Peru, Colombia**: Traditional use protected
- **United States**: DMT scheduled; ayahuasca technically illegal (though courts have protected religious use)
- **Canada**: Health Canada has provided exemptions for some contexts
- **Europe**: Variable; some countries have psychedelic research exemptions

### Harm Reduction Approach

Even where illegal, harm reduction principles suggest:
- Safety protocols should not depend on legal status
- Participant health and well-being are paramount
- Document safety practices
- Maintain appropriate medical support structures
