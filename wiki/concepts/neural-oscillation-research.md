# Neural Oscillation Key Studies

## Overview

This concept summarizes the core research establishing how psychedelics modulate neural oscillations (gamma, theta, coherence) and how these changes relate to therapeutic effects.

## Key Research Findings

### Gamma Oscillations (38-40 Hz)
**Significance**: Associated with ego-dissolution and meditation self-transcendence

**Key Studies**:
- Acosta-Urquidi QEEG study (2015): Acute fronto-parietal gamma increases during DMT
- Jefferson et al. (2023): 5-MeO-DMT promotes structural neural plasticity in mice

**Mechanism**: Gamma increases reflect "narrative inflection point" where consciousness disidentifies from thoughts

### Theta Oscillations (6-8 Hz)
**Significance**: Associated with anxiolytic effects, cognitive control, memory consolidation

**Key Studies**:
- Riga et al. (2018): 5-MeO-DMT alters theta in medial PFC and ventral hippocampus
- Winne et al. (2020): 5-MeO-DMT prevents anxiety-like behavior through theta modulation

**Mechanism**: Theta increases in frontal midline regions indicate relaxed alertness and parasympathetic activity

### Coherence Across Frequency Bands
**Significance**: Functional connectivity, network integration

**Key Finding**: Reversible increases in coherence following 5-MeO-DMT suggest network reorganization

## Clinical Implications

1. **For Addiction (AUD)**:
   - Increased theta coherence in abstinent alcoholics reflects enhanced top-down control
   - Gamma increases may mark the moment of ego-dissolution enabling new perspectives
   
2. **For Depression**:
   - Psilocybin produces sustained theta increases correlated with antidepressant effects
   - Theta may be a biomarker of treatment response

3. **For PTSD**:
   - Theta/gamma ratio changes associated with fear extinction
   - Coherence increases support emotional regulation
