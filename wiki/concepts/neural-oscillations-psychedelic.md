# Neural Oscillations and Psychedelic Therapy

## Overview

This concept explores how psychedelics modulate brain wave patterns (neural oscillations) and how these changes relate to therapeutic effects. Research from 5-MeO-DMT studies provides particularly clear evidence due to the compound's rapid onset and short duration, allowing precise measurement of acute neural effects.

## Key Frequency Bands

### Gamma (38-40 Hz)
- **Associated with**: Ego-dissolution, meditation self-dissolution
- **Location**: Fronto-parietal regions
- **Interpretation**: Neurological correlate of the "narrative inflection point" where individuals disidentify with thoughts, emotions, or behaviors
- **Clinical relevance**: May reflect the moment of psychological breakthrough

### Theta (6-8 Hz)
- **Associated with**: Anxiolytic effects, relaxed alertness, cognitive control
- **Location**: Frontal midline regions (PFC, ACC)
- **Interpretation**: State of relaxed alertness conducive to mental health
- **Clinical relevance**: Improved inhibitory control, reduced negative affect, decreased craving

### Coherence (cross-frequency)
- **Meaning**: Degree of coupling between frequency bands in different brain regions
- **Interpretation**: Functional connectivity indicator
- **Clinical relevance**: Reorganization of executive control networks → improved emotion regulation

## Meditation Parallels

Extensive research shows meditation also modulates these same frequencies:

| Band | Meditation Effect | Psychedelic Effect |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| Gamma | Increases during self-dissolution meditation | Acute increases |
| Theta | Anxiolytic, relaxed alertness | Increases in frontal midline |
| Coherence | Increased theta/alpha/gamma coherence | Reversible increase across bands |

This convergence suggests shared neural mechanisms between meditation and psychedelic-induced states.

## AUD Implications

Long-term abstinent alcoholics show higher theta and alpha coherence compared to controls, reflecting enhanced top-down executive control (inhibitory control, emotion regulation). 

5-MeO-DMT may reorganize executive control networks after a single dose, consistent with:
1. **Neuroplasticity hypothesis**: New neural pathways replace old patterns
2. **Default Mode Network reset**: Temporary reduction allows new perspectives
3. **Executive function improvement**: Better cognitive control over craving

## Research Findings from 5-MeO-DMT

EEG studies show acute increases in:
- Gamma → ego-dissolution marker
- Theta → cognitive control improvement
- Coherence across all frequency bands → network reorganization

These effects are reversible but may leave lasting changes through neuroplasticity.

## Why This Matters

Understanding neural oscillations helps explain:
- Why set and setting matter (pre-existing brain states influence outcomes)
- Why meditation practice potentiates psychedelic therapy
- How "ego-dissolution" translates to measurable brain activity
- Why some people respond better to treatment
