# Matthew W. Johnson

## Overview

Matthew W. Johnson is a pioneering researcher in psychedelic-assisted therapy, particularly for addiction treatment. He has conducted extensive research on 5-MeO-DMT, psilocybin, and the subjective effects necessary for therapeutic outcomes.

## Key Contributions

### 1. Psychedelic Treatment of Addiction
- Co-authored foundational reviews on classic hallucinogens for addiction (Bogenschutz & Johnson, 2016)
- Research showing psychedelic use leads to cessation/reduction in alcohol use

### 2. Subjective Effects Necessity Hypothesis
**Key Paper**: "The subjective effects of psychedelics are necessary for their enduring therapeutic effects" (Yaden & Griffiths, 2021 - Johnson was supervisor)

Johnson's framework argues:
- The quality of the mystical experience predicts outcomes
- Not pharmacology alone, but the experience itself drives change
- This is why set and setting are critical

### 3. 5-MeO-DMT Epidemiology
- Co-authored large survey study (n=515) on 5-MeO-DMT use patterns
- Found 66% of users reported improvements in alcoholism
- Showed mystical experience correlates with therapeutic outcomes

### 4. Twelve-Step Program Synergies
- Paper: "Classic psychedelics in the treatment of substance use disorder: potential synergies with twelve-step programs"
- Bridging psychedelic therapy with established recovery programs
