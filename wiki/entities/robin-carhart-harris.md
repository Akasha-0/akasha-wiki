# Robin Carhart-Harris

## Overview

Robin Carhart-Harris is a pioneering psychedelic researcher at Imperial College London who has led groundbreaking brain imaging studies and developed the influential REBUS model explaining how psychedelics produce therapeutic change.

## Key Contributions

### 1. REBUS Model
- Developed the RElaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics (REBUS) model
- Proposed psychedelics work by "relaxing" high-level priors about self and world
- Provided unified theoretical framework for psychedelic therapy

### 2. Brain Imaging Studies
- First psilocybin brain imaging studies (fMRI, EEG)
- Showed decreased activity in Default Mode Network (DMN)
- Demonstrated increased global brain connectivity during psychedelic states

### 3. Psilocybin for Depression
- Led clinical trials: "Psilocybin with psychological support for treatment-resistant depression"
- Showed rapid and sustained antidepressant effects
- Brain imaging revealed changes consistent with REBUS model

### 4. The Entropic Brain
- Proposed that psychedelics increase brain entropy (randomness/flexibility)
- Entropy increase correlates with subjective experience intensity
- Framework for understanding why mystical experiences occur
