# Jessica Dean

Jessica G. Dean is a researcher known for her work on endogenous DMT biosynthesis in the mammalian brain.

## Key Publication

### DMT in Mammalian Brain (2019)
**Dean JG et al. (2019).** "Biosynthesis and extracellular concentrations of N,N-dimethyltryptamine (DMT) in mammalian brain." *Scientific Reports* 9:9333.

This landmark study provided **substantial evidence of endogenous DMT in the rat brain**, addressing a major gap in the field. The findings include:

- DMT levels in the **pineal gland and visual cortex** comparable to other neuroamines (serotonin, dopamine)
- DMT is present at **levels akin to canonical neurotransmitters**
- A **sudden increase in DMT levels** was observed in rats during **cardiac arrest**

### Implications

This study demonstrated that:
1. DMT is produced **locally in the brain** (not just in peripheral tissues)
2. Brain DMT levels are **comparable to other established neurotransmitters**
3. DMT may be released during **extreme physiological states** (cardiac arrest), supporting the NDE hypothesis
