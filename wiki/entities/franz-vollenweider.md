# Franz X. Vollenweider

## Overview

Franz X. Vollenweider is a leading psychopharmacology researcher at University of Zurich who has conducted foundational research on how psychedelics affect the brain through serotonin receptors and large-scale brain networks.

## Key Research Contributions

### 1. Serotonin Receptor Mechanisms
- Research showing 5-HT2A receptor activation is necessary for psychedelic effects
- Showed that blocking 5-HT2A eliminates psychedelic experiences
- Demonstrated role of 5-HT1A in modulating psychedelic effects

### 2. Brain Network Effects
- Pioneered research on how psychedelics affect brain network connectivity
- Default Mode Network (DMN) suppression by psychedelics
- Increased global brain connectivity

### 3. Meditation Interactions
- Research on how psilocybin and meditation modulate similar brain networks
- The relaxation of Bayesian priors model
