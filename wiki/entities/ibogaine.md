# Ibogaine

## Overview

Ibogaine is a psychoactive alkaloid extracted from the root bark of the African shrub Tabernanthe iboga. It has a long history of use in Bwiti spiritual traditions in Africa and has attracted scientific interest for its potential to treat addiction, particularly opioid use disorder. Unlike other psychedelics, ibogaine produces a unique experience lasting 24-48 hours with distinct neuropharmacological properties.

## Pharmacology

### Receptor Targets
- **SERT modulators**: Serotonin transporter modulation
- **NMDA antagonists**: Glutamate receptor antagonism
- **Opioid receptor interactions**: Kappa and mu opioid receptor binding
- **Dopamine modulation**: Reduces dopamine release associated with drug reward

### Duration
- Acute effects: 24-48 hours
- Long half-life due to active metabolites
- Requires extended medical monitoring

## Clinical Applications

### Addiction Treatment
- **Opioid use disorder**: Most researched application
- **Alcohol use disorder**: Emerging evidence
- **Cocaine addiction**: Preliminary studies
- Reduces withdrawal symptoms
- Decreases craving intensity

### Mechanisms
- Interrupts addiction neurocircuitry
- Promotes psychological insight into addiction patterns
- May reset reward system homeostasis

## Safety Concerns

### Cardiac Risks
- **QT prolongation**: Cardiotoxicity documented
- Requires ECG monitoring before and during treatment
- 2026 scoping review: cardiac safety remains primary concern

### Medical Supervision Required
- Not suitable for unsupervised use
- Requires specialized clinical settings
- Contraindicated with certain cardiac conditions
