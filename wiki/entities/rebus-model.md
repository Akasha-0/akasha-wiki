# REBUS: The Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics Model

## Overview

The RElaxed Beliefs Under pSychedelics (REBUS) model is a theoretical framework proposing that psychedelics work primarily by weakening the precision weighting of high-level Bayesian priors—deep-seated beliefs about the self, world, and identity. This model provides a unified explanation for how diverse psychedelic experiences produce therapeutic change.

## The Bayesian Brain Framework

The brain is conceptualized as a prediction machine:
- **Priors**: Pre-existing beliefs/expectations about reality
- **Prediction errors**: Mismatch between predictions and sensory input
- **Precision weighting**: How much confidence we assign to predictions vs. sensory evidence

In depression, anxiety, and addiction:
- Rigid, overly precise priors dominate perception
- New information is distorted to fit existing beliefs
- Negative self-perception and avoidance behaviors persist

## How Psychedelics Change Beliefs

**REBUS proposes**:
1. **Relaxation of priors**: Psychedelics reduce the precision weighting of top-down predictions
2. **Increased prediction error signaling**: Bottom-up sensory information gains access
3. **New perspectives**: Beliefs can be revised based on actual experience
4. **Unification with experience**: Self-boundaries temporarily dissolve

## The Ego as a Prior

The sense of self (ego) is viewed as one of the most rigid priors:
- "I am my body/mind/history/roles"
- This prior filters all experience
- Ego dissolution = temporary relaxation of self-prior

**For AUD**: The prior "I am an addict" or "I need alcohol to cope" is weakened, allowing new self-concepts to form.

## Evidence Supporting REBUS

1. Neural oscillations (gamma) correlate with prior relaxation
2. DMN activity (self-referential processing) is suppressed
3. Mystical experiences involve temporary ego dissolution
4. Psychological flexibility increases post-psychedelic

## Clinical Implications

- Quality of mystical/ego-dissolution experience predicts outcomes (not just pharmacological effect)
- Set and setting matter because they influence which priors get challenged
- Integration work helps consolidate new priors
