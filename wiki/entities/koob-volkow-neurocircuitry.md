# Koob and Volkow Neurocircuitry Model

## Overview

George Koob and Nora Volkow's 2016 landmark paper "Neurobiology of Addiction: A Neurocircuitry Analysis" provides the foundational framework for understanding how addiction affects the brain. This model is cited extensively in 5-MeO-DMT and ayahuasca addiction research because it explains why psychedelic-assisted therapy can work.

## The Three Neurocircuitry Systems

Koob and Volkow identified three interconnected brain systems that are disrupted in addiction:

### 1. Incentive Salience (Basal Ganglia)
- **Dysfunction**: Hyperactivity of reward circuits, craving
- **Neurotransmitters**: Dopamine overflow, glutamate dysregulation
- **Clinical manifestation**: "Wanting" vs "Liking" - compulsive seeking

### 2. Negative Affect (Extended Amygdala)
- **Dysfunction**: Chronic negative emotional state, dysphoria
- **Neurotransmitters**: CRF, dynorphin, norepinephrine
- **Clinical manifestation**: Anxiety, irritability, emotional pain
- **Connection**: Self-medication hypothesis - using substances to escape negative affect

### 3. Executive Function (Prefrontal Cortex)
- **Dysfunction**: Impaired inhibitory control, decision-making
- **Neurotransmitters**: Glutamate/GABA imbalance
- **Clinical manifestation**: Inability to stop use despite consequences

## Why This Matters for Psychedelic Therapy

**Traditional addiction treatment targets these systems pharmacologically:**
- Dopamine antagonists (incentive salience)
- Anxiolytics/antidepressants (negative affect)
- But none address the fundamental neurocircuitry reorganization

**Psychedelic approach:**
- Ego-dissolution allows re-perception of self and behavior
- Neural oscillations (theta/gamma) may reset executive control networks
- Mystical experiences can directly alter the negative affect system

## Connection to AUD

In Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD), these three systems form a vicious cycle:

```
Negative Affect → Incentive Salience → Executive Dysfunction
     ↑                                          ↓
     ←←←←←←←←←←← DISEASE STATE ←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←
```
