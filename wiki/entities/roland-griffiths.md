# Roland R. Griffiths

## Overview

Roland R. Griffiths was a foundational researcher at Johns Hopkins University who led critical research on psilocybin's subjective effects, mystical experience measurement (MEQ), and therapeutic applications for addiction and depression.

**Note**: Dr. Griffiths passed away in 2023, but his research legacy continues through the Johns Hopkins Center for Psychedelic & Consciousness Research.

## Key Scientific Contributions

### 1. Development of the Mystical Experience Questionnaire (MEQ)
- Created the MEQ30/MEQ43 scales to measure mystical experiences
- Enabled rigorous research linking experience quality to outcomes
- Showed that mystical experience score is the best predictor of therapeutic benefit

### 2.Psilocybin for Depression and Addiction
- Landmark studies showing psilocybin reduces depression and anxiety
- Research on cigarette cessation (35% quit rate at 6 months)
- Cancer anxiety studies showing lasting benefits

### 3. Subjective Effects Research
- Demonstrated dose-dependent subjective effects of psychedelics
- Showed mystical-type experiences occur reliably with classic psychedelics
- Research on meditation-psychedelic synergies

### 4. Safety Research
- Established safety profile for psilocybin administration
- Showed adverse effects are generally transient and resolve spontaneously

## Legacy at Johns Hopkins
- Center for Psychedelic & Consciousness Research continues his work
- Training the next generation of psychedelic researchers
- Published safety protocols now used globally
